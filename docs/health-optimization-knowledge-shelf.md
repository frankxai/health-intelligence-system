# Health Optimization Knowledge Shelf

**Evidence checked:** 2026-06-22  
**Scope:** books, guidelines, and public sources that can inform general wellness questions, habit design, and clinician/professional conversations.  
**Safety boundary:** Books and general sources are educational inputs, not personal medical instructions.

## Source Hierarchy

1. Clinician or qualified professional guidance for the person.
2. Official public-health guidance and clinical/public institutions.
3. Systematic reviews or high-quality research when needed.
4. Books and expert frameworks as idea generators.
5. Personal experiments only inside ordinary wellness boundaries.

## Official Baselines

| Domain | Source |
| --- | --- |
| Physical activity | Physical Activity Guidelines for Americans, WHO physical activity fact sheet |
| Nutrition | CDC nutrition, Nutrition.gov, local dietitian guidance |
| Sleep | CDC sleep resources and clinician evaluation when sleep is impaired |
| Appointment questions | AHRQ Question Builder |
| Records access | HHS HIPAA, ONC information blocking, EU and country portals |

## Book Shelf For Idea Mining

| Theme | Book/example | How HIS should use it |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Longevity operating mindset | Peter Attia, `Outlive` | Generate questions and long-term healthspan categories, not disease protocols |
| Exercise anthropology | Daniel Lieberman, `Exercised` | Build movement literacy and reduce exercise myths |
| Sleep literacy | Matthew Walker, `Why We Sleep` | Create sleep hygiene questions and logs; do not diagnose sleep disorders |
| Mobility and durability | Kelly and Juliet Starrett, `Built to Move` | Build mobility check-ins and low-risk movement habits |
